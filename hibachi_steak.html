Hibachi Steak

Hibachi steak is is an enjoyable bit of Japanese cuisene. The dish consists of steak grilled on a flat metal pan and marrinated with soy sauce. It is typically served with fried rice and carrots and/or mushrooms. The term 'hibachi' is something of a misnomer. The cooking style described is called Teppanyaki. The term hibachi refers to a type of cookware, but has often been conflated in the west to refer to the cooking style.

What follows is a brief recipe and ingredient list for a gluten free hibachi style steak preperation. It has been tweeked a bit for cooking in a westen kitchen and I myself have cooked on a few occasions. To cook this dish in a gluten free manner is fairly straight forward and consists primarily of swtiching standard soy sauce out for Tamari sauce. The recipe, to be sourced properly at a later date, is derived from The Spruce Eats https://www.thespruceeats.com/grilled-hibachi-steak-recipe-336291.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 a cup of Tamari sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of granulated sugar
  • 2 Table spoons of minced garlic (equivalent to 1 clove)
  • At least 2 teaspoons grated fresh ginger, a full tablespoon is recomended
  • 1/2 teaspoon of white pepper
  • 1lb of steak, boneless preferably as 3-4 seperate cuts
  • 4 teaspoons vegetable oil

Steps for preperation.

  1. To prepare the sauce combine the Tamari sauce, white vinegar, sugar, garlic, ginger, and white pepper and boil on low heat for 3 minutes while stiring.
  2. Prapare a gril on medium high heat.
  3. Gril the steak unti grid marks start to form but the steak remains rare.
  4. Chop the steak into bit size cubes.
  5. Preheat a skillet over medium heat and then add the oil, mushrooms, and steak.
  6. Cook for 3 minutes while tossing, the mushrooms should start to brown when ready.
  7. Add the sauce and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes
  8. Garnish with asdf if you prefer
